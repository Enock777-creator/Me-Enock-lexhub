Understanding the Terms "Special" and "Bis" in Legal Drafting

In law, the term "special" often refers to something that applies under specific conditions or to a limited group, such as a "special law" that overrides a general one in certain cases. Meanwhile, "bis" is a Latin term meaning "twice" and is used in numbering articles (e.g., Article 5 bis) to insert a new rule without renumbering the whole document.
